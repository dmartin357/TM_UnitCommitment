The goal of this alternate stage 1 algorighm will just be to implement random cuts until the maximum power capability (all units running at 100%) is above a threshold and the minimum power 
capability (all units commited but operating at the lowest power level) is below a threshold. The thresholds will be based on renewable uncertainty. For example, if the maximum demand with 
renewable generation is set to 100MW and the minimum demand with renewable generation is set to 50MW with an expected demand with renewable generation set to 75MW, then the maximum 
threshold might be 100MW plus some percentage of the expected demand and the minimum threshold might be 50MW minus some percentage of the expected demand. This algorithm will randomly cut 
units until the maximum or minimum cumulative power output violate their respective thresholds. This iteration (the cuts prior to the violation) will be considered a sample, and the goal 
will be to estimate the percentage that a unit was committed during a time period and subsequently how much it was committed throughout the time horizon.
